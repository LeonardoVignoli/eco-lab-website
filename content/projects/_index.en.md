LEC lab is involved in research and conservation projects ranging from basic ecology
to active management of natural populations.

## Research and conservation projects

### EOLIZARD
Ecological and conservation-oriented research on lizard populations, focusing on
spatial structure, behaviour and population dynamics in natural and human-modified
environments.

### Apennine yellow-bellied toad (*Bombina pachypus*)
In situ and ex situ conservation of a highly threatened species, including habitat
restoration (creation of ponds, restoration of springs and watering troughs), captive
breeding and restocking and reintroduction programs.

### Sardinian brook salamander (*Euproctus platycephalus*)
Conservation project on this Sardinian endemic species, including population monitoring,
distribution analyses, captive breeding and a pilot reintroduction project in the
Settefratelli Mountains (CA).

### Wetland monitoring using eDNA
Assessment of aquatic invertebrate and vertebrate diversity, with a focus on amphibians
and odonates, integrating traditional surveys with environmental DNA techniques.

### Biologging in amphibians and reptiles
Use of miniaturised devices (triaxial accelerometers, magnetometers, temperature sensors)
to reconstruct spatial ecology and hidden movements of small ectothermic tetrapods.

### TROPHYSPEC
Study of trophic ecology and individual specialisation in ectothermic vertebrates, analysing
dietary niche at both population and individual levels across different ecological contexts.

### Population genetics in urodele amphibians
Genetic analyses to investigate population structure, connectivity and evolutionary
dynamics of salamander populations.

### Native–invasive species interactions
Assessment of ecological impacts of invasive species (e.g. *Aquarana catesbeiana*) on
native communities and evaluation of management strategies.

### Conservation through artificial wetland management
Evaluation of the role of artificial ponds and wetlands in the conservation of threatened
amphibian species.

### Monitoring of Sardinian cave salamanders
Development of monitoring protocols for *Speleomantes sarrabusensis* (CR) and other
endangered Sardinian cave salamanders.

### PRIN SPIA
“Stick insects as Parthenogenetic Indicators of Air pollution” – use of clonal organisms as
bioindicators for monitoring air pollution in terrestrial environments.
