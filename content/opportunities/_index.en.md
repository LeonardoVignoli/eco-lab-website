## Opportunities for BSc, MSc Theses and Internships

The ECB Lab regularly offers opportunities for BSc and MSc theses, as well as curricular internships, within its ongoing research and conservation projects.

Students may be involved in fieldwork, ecological monitoring, data collection and analysis, wildlife surveys, statistical analyses, and the development of independent research projects. Activities are often carried out in collaboration with research institutions, protected areas, and national and international conservation partners.

## Conservation of the Aeolian Wall Lizard (LIFE EOLIZARD)

Within the framework of the European project LIFE EOLIZARD, dedicated to the conservation of the Aeolian wall lizard (*Podarcis raffonei*), thesis and internship opportunities are available on a variety of topics related to island ecology and conservation biology.

Potential research topics include:

- population monitoring and demographic analyses;
- spatial ecology and habitat use;
- impacts of invasive species on island biodiversity;
- evaluation of conservation actions and management strategies;
- ecological and demographic data analysis;
- science communication and conservation outreach.

Activities may include fieldwork in the Aeolian Islands and direct participation in conservation actions implemented within the LIFE project.

## Biologging and Animal Tracking in Amphibians and Reptiles

The ECB Lab develops and applies innovative biologging and animal-tracking techniques to investigate the ecology and behaviour of amphibians and reptiles.

Available thesis and internship projects may focus on:

- VHF radiotelemetry;
- the use of accelerometers, magnetometers, and GPS devices;
- movement ecology and space-use analyses;
- activity patterns and behavioural ecology;
- development and validation of tracking methodologies for small vertebrates;
- applications of biologging technologies to wildlife conservation.

Students will have the opportunity to acquire practical and analytical skills that are increasingly valuable in the fields of movement ecology, wildlife monitoring, and conservation science.

## How to Apply

Students interested in joining the laboratory are encouraged to contact Leonardo Vignoli at [leonardo.vignoli@uniroma3.it](mailto:leonardo.vignoli@uniroma3.it), attaching a short CV and a brief description of their scientific interests.

Research activities and thesis topics are defined according to the student's interests, background, and the ongoing projects of the laboratory. We particularly welcome motivated students interested in field ecology, conservation biology, quantitative ecology, and innovative wildlife-monitoring technologies.
