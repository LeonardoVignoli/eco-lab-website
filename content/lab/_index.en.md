**From field research to conservation action**

The **LEC — Lab for Ecology & Conservation** is part of the
Department of Sciences at Roma Tre University.

Our research activities focus on the ecological processes that regulate
the distribution and functioning of animal communities, with particular
attention to amphibians and reptiles, endangered species, and invasive species.

The laboratory integrates approaches from behavioral ecology, functional ecology,
population genetics, and ecotoxicology, with the aim of developing
management and conservation strategies based on solid scientific evidence.

Activities include field research, long-term monitoring,
laboratory experiments, and collaborations with management bodies, protected areas,
and national and international institutions.


## Research

The **LEC - Lab for Ecology & Conservation** carries out research on the ecological
processes shaping the distribution, abundance and functioning of animal communities,
with a strong focus on amphibians and reptiles, threatened species and invasive species.

Our goal is to integrate basic and applied research to support effective biodiversity
conservation strategies.

### Research topics

- Population and community ecology
- Functional and behavioural ecology
- Ecotoxicology
- Conservation biology
- Native-invasive species interactions

### Methods and approaches

- Long-term field monitoring
- Laboratory and field experiments
- Tracking and biologging technologies
- Population genetic analyses
- Molecular methods (eDNA) for biodiversity monitoring

### Collaborations

The lab collaborates with universities, research institutes, protected areas and local
authorities, contributing to national and international research and conservation projects.
